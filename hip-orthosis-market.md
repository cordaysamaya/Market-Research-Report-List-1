<p><p>The hip orthosis market is primarily categorized into two types: fixed and wearable. Fixed type orthoses provide rigid support and immobilization, aiding in recovery from fractures or surgeries by restricting movement. They are commonly used in clinical settings. In contrast, wearable type orthoses are designed for enhanced mobility and comfort, allowing patients to engage in daily activities while offering necessary support. These are often favored for rehabilitation purposes, promoting recovery with a focus on functionality and ease of use.</p></p>
