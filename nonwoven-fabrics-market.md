<p><p>The nonwoven fabrics market encompasses various manufacturing processes, including dry-laid, spunmelt, wet-laid, and others. Dry-laid fabrics are produced by carding or air-laying fibers, resulting in lightweight materials often used in hygiene and medical applications. Spunmelt combines spunbond and meltblown technologies to create strong, versatile fabrics used in filtration and protective clothing. Wet-laid processes use water to process fibers, creating durable and absorbent materials, typically utilized in wipes or geotextiles. Other methods may include needle-punching and thermobonding.</p></p>
