<p><ul><li>Chemical Indicators</li><li>Genetically Encoded Indicators</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Calcium indicators are crucial tools for monitoring intracellular calcium dynamics. The market is divided into two primary types: chemical indicators and genetically encoded indicators. Chemical indicators are dyes or small molecules that bind to calcium ions, changing fluorescence or absorbance properties for detection. In contrast, genetically encoded indicators are proteins engineered to respond to calcium fluctuations, typically used in live-cell imaging. Both types serve essential roles in neuroscience, cell biology, and pharmacology, facilitating the study of cellular processes and signaling pathways.</p></p>
