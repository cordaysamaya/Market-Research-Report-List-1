<p><p>The rock sugar market can be divided into two main types: monocrystalline and natural rock sugar. Monocrystalline rock sugar features uniform, crystal-like formations that offer aesthetic appeal and slow dissolution, making it popular in culinary uses. Natural rock sugar, typically derived from sugar cane or beet, retains mineral content and natural flavors, enhancing its appeal as a healthier alternative. Both types cater to various consumer preferences in the food and beverage industry, contributing to their distinct market segments.</p></p>
