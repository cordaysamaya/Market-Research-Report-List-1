<p><p>The truck alloy wheel market is primarily categorized into three types: casting, forging, and other methods. </p><p>Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold, creating intricate designs with good strength but less durability. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping metal through compressive forces, resulting in lighter, stronger wheels that can withstand higher stress. Other methods may include hybrid processes or advanced technologies like 3D printing, offering unique advantages in customization and performance. These distinctions cater to diverse needs in the trucking industry.</p></p>
