<p><p>The electroceutical market is divided into two main types: implantable and non-implantable devices. Implantable electroceuticals are surgically placed devices that deliver electrical stimulation to target areas of the body, often used for chronic pain management or neurological conditions. Non-implantable devices, on the other hand, are external devices that provide therapeutic electrical stimulation without requiring surgery, often used in rehabilitation or pain relief. Both types aim to harness electrical signals to influence biological functions and improve health outcomes.</p></p>
