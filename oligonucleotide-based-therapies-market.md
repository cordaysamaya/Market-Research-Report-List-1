<p><p>The oligonucleotide-based therapies market includes several types: antisense oligonucleotides, aptamers, and others. Antisense oligonucleotides are designed to bind to specific RNA molecules, inhibiting gene expression and offering potential treatments for genetic disorders and cancers. Aptamers are short, single-stranded nucleic acids that can selectively bind to target proteins, functioning as therapeutic agents or diagnostics. Other emerging technologies include RNA interference and exon skipping strategies, which aim to modify gene expression or splicing for treating various diseases.</p></p>
